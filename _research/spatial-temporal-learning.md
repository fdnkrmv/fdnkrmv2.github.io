Spatial-temporal learning is central to my research. I study how machine learning models can represent patterns that vary across geography, time, and social context, particularly when the underlying environment changes over time.

This includes modelling temporal drift, learning transferable representations across locations, and designing approaches that remain useful when historical relationships no longer hold exactly in the future.
